Recommendations & Feedback

Review each key recommendation and submit your input. All suggestions are securely collected for analysis.

Adopt a unified, risk-informed Umbrella ToC that integrates the HDP nexus and system-strengthening. It makes megatrends and risk-to-budget choices explicit, clarifies shared outcomes and cross-thematic dependencies (HRH, supply chains, QA/supervision, referrals), and positions population data to steer policy and finance built to technical quality (clear assumptions, indicators, learning loops) as a live strategic-management tool.

Priority: High
Rationale: One Umbrella ToC gives the country programme a single, shared map for joint strategic monitoring and decision-making, replacing siloed thematic views with a common results frame that everyone co-owns. It aligns AWPs and budgets to the same outcomes and risks, enables timely cross-team course-correction, and anchors performance dialogues with government and partners. In periods of anticipated change (policy, climate, financing), the shared ToC also drives structured adaptive management—with pre-agreed triggers, scenario options, and a routine ToC review cadence—so the office’s proven agility becomes systematic practice.
Operational Implications:
  • Run a 4–6 week co-design sprint mapping HDP phases, megatrends, system functions, indicators, and risks.
  • Map all outputs/AWPs/budget lines to ToC; full alignment by Q3–Q4; gate new pilots until mapped.
  • Monthly joint reviews with ToC Steward, Programme+Ops+M&E+Humanitarian, version control ToC changes.
  • Minimal dashboard: 10–12 indicators, system floors tracked quarterly.
  • Pre-set contingency/pre-positioning lines, scenario playbooks, bridge financing during transition year.
  • Update all IP MoUs/ToRs to Umbrella ToC; joint ToC review on assumptions/risks & equity results.

Standardize → budgetize proven SRHR/GBV/A&Y models and data. Turn validated models and standards (midwifery protocols, GBV clinical pathways, referral closure, DHIS2/PD products) into the national routine by codifying them in norms/QA tools and assigning budget codes and packaging them for policy uptake (gazette/indicator compendium) and cross-country replication via South-South & Triangular Cooperation (SSTC).

Priority: High
Rationale: Scaling is durable when excellence is standardized, budgetized, and codified so ministries can adopt beyond pilot sites. Linking PD evidence to budget classifications turns analysis into allocation and sharpens equity (including adolescents and PwD). SSTC provides the diffusion engine (peer learning, cost benchmarking, pooled procurement), while systematization of good practices and lessons converts tacit knowledge into concise, reusable packages, accelerating policy uptake, quality, and replication.
Operational Implications:
  • Define standard package: one spec per model (norms/QA, indicators, referral closure).
  • Map to money (Q2): confirm budget codes/AWP lines for each package.
  • QA toolkit + simple fidelity score; supervision, DHIS2 completeness, equity fields filled.
  • Replication kit: policy brief, cost note, SOPs, indicator addendum, short explainer; policy circular/gazette and SSTC twinning.
  • Quarterly harvest of good practices/lessons → validation → short case notes → updates to SOPs/indicator compendium & kits.
  • Adoption dashboard on adoption–quality–equity, with thresholds for scale-up.
  • Require IP use of national QA toolkit and submission of fidelity score.
